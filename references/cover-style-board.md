# Cover Style Board: NeuroMed-XAI Benchmark

This style board summarizes cover-art patterns observed from Nature-family and IEEE biomedical AI/neuroengineering visuals. Do not copy protected cover art into the repository; use these as design principles.

## Reference Sources

- Nature Biomedical Engineering / Nature Medicine / Nature Machine Intelligence visual language: single symbolic scientific object, dramatic lighting, high contrast, careful negative space.
- IEEE JBHI / TBME graphical-abstract language: signal-processing pipelines, electrode maps, model-explanation overlays, engineered clarity.
- Journal of Neural Engineering and biomedical AI figures: topographic maps, saliency, time-frequency panels, spatio-temporal graphs.

## Style Principles

- Make the central subject an interpretable evidence object: a brain/topography intersecting with EEG traces and attribution maps.
- Use a clinical-research palette: deep indigo/black, cyan signal, magenta attention, amber disease-relevant evidence.
- Show benchmark rigor through repeated signal strips, split partitions, and report layers.
- Use volumetric glow and translucent overlays, but keep the science legible.
- Avoid clutter: three evidence layers are enough.

## Composition Target

`EEG recording -> benchmark split -> model evidence -> interpretable report`

The final asset should feel like a research cover image for an open-source biomedical AI platform, not a hospital advertisement.
